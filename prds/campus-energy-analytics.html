Mini Wind Energy Harvester

A fan-driven wind energy test setup built to study how magnets, coils, rectification, and electrical loading affect the conversion of airflow into usable DC power.

Problem

Small-scale energy harvesting concepts are often explained theoretically, but they become more valuable when tested through real measurements, conversion stages, and efficiency tradeoffs.

Objective

Build a bench-scale system that generates AC output from fan-driven motion, converts it to DC, and records enough data to compare performance under different conditions.

Core Requirements

  • Generate measurable AC output using magnets and coils
  • Implement AC-to-DC conversion through rectification
  • Use Arduino for analog data acquisition and a DMM for validation
  • Log and analyze readings with Python and Excel
  • Compare the effects of airflow, magnetic induction, and load resistance

Tools

  • Arduino
  • Python and Excel
  • Digital multimeter
  • Electrical measurement and circuit components

Success Criteria

  • Stable data capture across multiple test conditions
  • Clear comparison between generated AC and rectified DC output
  • Useful plots and calculations that explain system performance
